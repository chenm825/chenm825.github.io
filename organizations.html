The Real Issue

In June 2019, there were 60,849 homeless people sleeping in a New York City shelter per night.
The number of homeless New Yorkers who sleep in shelters each night is now 67 percent higher than it was 10 years ago; in fact, homelessness in New York has reached its highest levels since the Great Depression.
Although there are a number shelters available in New York City, thousands of unsheltered homeless people sleep on the streets each night.
